Description

The Personal Management merit badge teaches Scouts essential life skills in managing their time, money, and resources effectively. Scouts learn about budgeting, saving, investing, and making informed financial decisions. This merit badge emphasizes practical skills that will help Scouts become responsible adults and make sound financial choices throughout their lives.

Requirements

  1. Do the following:
    • Choose an item that your family might want to purchase that is considered a major expense.
    • Write a plan that tells how your family would save money for the purchase identified in requirement 1a.
      • Discuss the plan with your merit badge counselor.
      • Discuss the plan with your family.
      • If the plan is approved by your family, follow the plan for three months.
    • Complete the following requirements after three months of saving:
      • Create a record of your saving and spending for three months.
      • Explain to your merit badge counselor what you learned about saving and spending habits.
      • Explain to your merit badge counselor what you learned about yourself and your family's spending habits.
  2. Do the following:
    • Prepare a budget reflecting your expected income (allowance, gifts, wages), expenses, and savings. Track your actual income, expenses, and savings for 13 consecutive weeks. (You may use the forms provided in this pamphlet, devise your own, or use a computer generated version.) When complete, present the results to your merit badge counselor.
    • Compare expected income with expected expenses.
      • If expenses exceed income, determine steps to balance your budget.
      • If income exceeds expenses, state how you would use the excess money (new goal, savings).
  3. Do the following:
    • Choose a career you might want to enter after high school or college graduation.
    • Research the limitations and advantages of this career.
    • Explain the education and training required for this career.
    • Describe the starting and advancement opportunities for this career.
    • Tell what the future employment outlook is for this career.
    • Describe a written plan you would use for getting education or training required for this career.
    • Explain to your merit badge counselor what you have learned about this career and what you would do to prepare for it.
  4. Do the following:
    • Explain to your merit badge counselor the following:
      • Common types of investments (savings accounts, certificates of deposit, stocks, bonds, and mutual funds)
      • Risk and return and the importance of diversification
      • Why it is important to be a wise consumer of financial products and services
      • The difference between saving and investing
      • The concepts of return on investment and risk
      • The concept of diversification of investments
      • The concept of the time value of money
      • The concept of compound interest and how it affects your savings and investments
    • Explain to your merit badge counselor the following:
      • What a loan is, what interest is, and how the annual percentage rate (APR) measures the true cost of a loan
      • The different ways to borrow money
      • The differences between a charge card, debit card, and credit card
      • Credit reports and how personal responsibility can affect your credit report
      • Ways to reduce or eliminate debt
  5. Do the following:
    • Explain to your merit badge counselor the following:
      • The concept of insurance and its importance
      • The following types of insurance: health, life, homeowner's/renter's, auto, and liability
      • The concept of risk management
      • The concept of cost of insurance
      • The importance of having adequate insurance
    • Explain to your merit badge counselor the following:
      • The concept of financial planning and its importance
      • The concept of budgeting and its importance
      • The concept of tax planning and its importance
      • The concept of retirement planning and its importance
      • The concept of estate planning and its importance
  6. Do the following:
    • Explain to your merit badge counselor the following:
      • The concept of financial planning and its importance
      • The concept of budgeting and its importance
      • The concept of tax planning and its importance
      • The concept of retirement planning and its importance
      • The concept of estate planning and its importance
    • Explain to your merit badge counselor the following:
      • The concept of financial planning and its importance
      • The concept of budgeting and its importance
      • The concept of tax planning and its importance
      • The concept of retirement planning and its importance
      • The concept of estate planning and its importance
  7. Do the following:
    • Explain to your merit badge counselor the following:
      • The concept of financial planning and its importance
      • The concept of budgeting and its importance
      • The concept of tax planning and its importance
      • The concept of retirement planning and its importance
      • The concept of estate planning and its importance
    • Explain to your merit badge counselor the following:
      • The concept of financial planning and its importance
      • The concept of budgeting and its importance
      • The concept of tax planning and its importance
      • The concept of retirement planning and its importance
      • The concept of estate planning and its importance

Pro Tip: For requirement 1, choose a realistic savings goal that your family can achieve within three months. This will help you learn about saving while keeping the project manageable. Use a spreadsheet or budgeting app to track your progress, and don't forget to include unexpected expenses in your planning.
