Description

The Personal Fitness merit badge helps Scouts develop and maintain a healthy lifestyle through physical fitness, proper nutrition, and good habits. This merit badge encourages Scouts to set personal fitness goals and work towards achieving them.

Requirements

  1. Physical Examination
    • Complete a physical examination by a licensed health-care provider
    • Get approval to begin a fitness program
    • Document your current fitness level
  2. Fitness Assessment
    • Complete the BSA Physical Fitness Test
    • Record your results for:
      • Push-ups
      • Sit-ups
      • Pull-ups
      • 1-mile run/walk
      • Body composition
  3. 12-Week Fitness Program
    • Design a 12-week fitness program
    • Include exercises for:
      • Cardiorespiratory endurance
      • Muscular strength
      • Muscular endurance
      • Flexibility
    • Track your progress weekly
  4. Nutrition Plan
    • Create a nutrition plan
    • Learn about:
      • Basic food groups
      • Portion sizes
      • Healthy eating habits
      • Hydration
  5. Lifestyle Changes
    • Identify unhealthy habits
    • Develop strategies to improve them
    • Track your progress
    • Document challenges and solutions
  6. Final Assessment
    • Retake the BSA Physical Fitness Test
    • Compare results with initial assessment
    • Document improvements
    • Set new fitness goals

Pro Tip: For requirement 3, start with a manageable fitness program and gradually increase intensity. This will help you stay motivated and avoid injury. Consider working with a physical education teacher or fitness trainer to develop your program.

Pro Tips

  1. Start Slow
    • Begin with manageable goals
    • Gradually increase intensity
    • Listen to your body
    • Rest when needed
  2. Stay Consistent
    • Schedule regular workout times
    • Find activities you enjoy
    • Track your progress
    • Celebrate small victories
  3. Nutrition Matters
    • Plan meals ahead
    • Stay hydrated
    • Eat balanced meals
    • Limit processed foods
  4. Safety First
    • Warm up properly
    • Use correct form
    • Stay within your limits
    • Get proper rest
