Description

The Citizenship in Society merit badge is a unique opportunity for Scouts to explore diversity, equity, and inclusion while developing their leadership skills. This merit badge helps Scouts understand how to be an upstander and make a positive impact in their communities.

Requirements

  1. Research and Discussion
    • Research and define key terms: diversity, equity, inclusion, ethical leadership, and upstander
    • Discuss with your counselor how these concepts relate to Scouting values
  2. Document Review
    • Review the Scout Oath and Scout Law
    • Explain how they relate to citizenship in society
    • Discuss how these values help create inclusive environments
  3. Personal Experience
    • Share a time when you experienced discrimination
    • Describe how you felt and how you responded
    • Discuss what you learned from the experience
  4. Community Impact
    • Identify a community issue related to diversity, equity, or inclusion
    • Research the issue and develop a plan to address it
    • Take action to implement your plan
  5. Leadership Project
    • Plan and lead a discussion or activity that promotes understanding of diversity
    • Include at least three different perspectives
    • Document the outcomes and lessons learned
  6. Ethical Decision Making
    • Describe a situation where you had to make an ethical decision
    • Explain your thought process and the outcome
    • Discuss what you learned about leadership
  7. Documentation and Reflection
    • Document your work on this merit badge
    • Reflect on what you learned about yourself and others
    • Share your insights with your counselor

Pro Tip: For requirement 5, consider organizing a diversity workshop or discussion group at your school or community center. This can provide valuable experience in leadership while making a positive impact in your community.

Pro Tips

  1. Be Open-Minded
    • Approach discussions with curiosity and respect
    • Listen actively to different perspectives
    • Be willing to learn from others' experiences
  2. Document Everything
    • Keep detailed notes of your research
    • Record your thoughts and reflections
    • Take photos or videos of your activities (with permission)
  3. Choose Meaningful Projects
    • Select issues that matter to your community
    • Involve diverse stakeholders
    • Focus on sustainable solutions
  4. Practice Active Listening
    • Give others your full attention
    • Ask clarifying questions
    • Reflect back what you've heard
