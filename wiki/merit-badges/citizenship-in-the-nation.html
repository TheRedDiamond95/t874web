Description

The Citizenship in the Nation merit badge helps Scouts understand what it means to be a good citizen of the United States of America. Scouts learn about the rights, duties, and obligations of citizenship, and how these are reflected in our Constitution and Bill of Rights.

Requirements

  1. Explain what citizenship in the nation means and what it takes to be a good citizen of this country.
  2. Do TWO of the following:
    • Visit a place that is listed as a National Historic Landmark or that is on the National Register of Historic Places.
    • Tour your state capitol building or the U.S. Capitol.
    • Tour a federal facility.
    • Choose a national monument that interests you.
  3. Watch the national evening news five days in a row OR read the front page of a major daily newspaper five days in a row.
  4. Discuss each of the following documents with your counselor:
    • Declaration of Independence
    • Preamble to the Constitution
    • Constitution
    • Bill of Rights
    • Amendments to the Constitution
  5. List the six functions of government as noted in the preamble to the Constitution.
  6. Name your representatives in the United States Congress.
  7. Write a letter about a national issue and send it to one of these elected officials.

Pro Tip: Start working on requirement 3 (news watching/reading) early, as it takes five consecutive days to complete. Also, consider visiting your state capitol or a national monument during a troop outing to fulfill requirement 2.
