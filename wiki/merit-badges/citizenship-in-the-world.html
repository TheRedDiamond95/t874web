Description

The Citizenship in the World merit badge helps Scouts understand their role in the global community. Scouts learn about international relationships, world organizations, and how they can be good global citizens. This merit badge emphasizes the importance of understanding different cultures and international issues.

Requirements

  1. Explain what citizenship in the world means to you and what you think it takes to be a good world citizen.
  2. Explain how one becomes a citizen in the United States, and explain the rights, duties, and obligations of U.S. citizenship.
  3. Do the following:
    • Pick a current world event.
    • Explain how this event affects you and your family.
    • Explain how this event affects the world.
  4. Pick two of the following organizations and describe their role in the world:
    • The United Nations
    • The World Court
    • World Organization of the Scout Movement
    • The World Health Organization
    • Amnesty International
    • The International Committee of the Red Cross
    • Care (formerly Cooperative for American Relief Everywhere)
  5. Explain international law and how it differs from national law.
  6. Do TWO of the following:
    • Explain international trade and how it affects the economy of the United States.
    • Describe the roles of the following in carrying out the UN's mission:
      • The UN General Assembly
      • UN Security Council
      • UN International Court of Justice
      • UN Economic and Social Council
    • Identify at least five different international problems and describe how they affect the world.
  7. Do ONE of the following:
    • Interview someone who has lived in another country.
    • Participate in an international event in your area.
    • Visit with a student or Scout from another country.
    • Write to a pen pal in another country.

Pro Tip: For requirement 7, consider reaching out to your local university's international student office or community organizations that work with immigrants. They can help connect you with people from other countries for interviews or pen pal opportunities.
