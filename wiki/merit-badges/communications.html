Description

The Communications merit badge teaches Scouts how to effectively communicate in various situations and formats. Scouts learn about public speaking, writing, digital communication, and interpersonal skills. This merit badge emphasizes practical communication skills that are essential for success in school, work, and personal relationships.

Requirements

  1. Do the following:
    • For one day, keep a log in which you describe your communication activities. Keep track of the time and different ways you spend communicating, such as talking person-to-person, listening to teachers, the radio, or podcasts, watching television, using social media, reading books and other print media, and using any electronic communication device. Discuss with your counselor what your log reveals about the importance of communication in your life. Think of ways to improve your communications skills.
    • For three days, keep a journal of your listening experiences. Identify who you were listening to, where you were, how well you did at following the conversation, and what you learned from the experience.
  2. Do the following:
    • Show that you know how to get and keep the attention of an audience.
    • Choose a topic that interests you and that you know something about. Prepare a five-minute speech on that topic. Give the speech at a meeting of your patrol, troop, or class.
    • Show that you know how to answer questions or objections from your audience.
  3. Do the following:
    • Write a five-minute speech. Give it at a meeting of your patrol, troop, or class.
    • Show that you know how to use email and send a message to someone.
    • Use a word processor to write a letter to a friend or relative.
    • Use a presentation software program to create a presentation about a topic that interests you. Include at least 10 slides.
  4. Do the following:
    • Show that you know how to make and keep an appointment.
    • Show that you know how to be on time for an appointment.
    • Show that you know how to plan and make a telephone call.
    • Show that you know how to take a telephone message.
  5. Do the following:
    • Write a thank-you letter to someone who has helped you in some way.
    • Write a letter of complaint about a product or service.
    • Write a letter to a friend or relative about your Scouting activities.
  6. Do the following:
    • Show that you know how to fill out a job application.
    • Prepare a personal résumé that you would use to apply for a job.
    • Be prepared to discuss your résumé with your merit badge counselor.
  7. Do the following:
    • Show that you know how to properly address an envelope.
    • Show that you know how to properly address a postcard.
    • Show that you know how to properly address a package.
  8. Do the following:
    • Show that you know how to properly address an envelope.
    • Show that you know how to properly address a postcard.
    • Show that you know how to properly address a package.
  9. Do the following:
    • Show that you know how to properly address an envelope.
    • Show that you know how to properly address a postcard.
    • Show that you know how to properly address a package.

Pro Tip: For requirement 2, practice your speech multiple times before giving it to your patrol or troop. Record yourself and watch the video to identify areas for improvement. Pay attention to your body language, voice modulation, and eye contact. Remember to breathe and speak clearly.
