Description

The Hiking merit badge teaches Scouts essential skills for safe and enjoyable hiking adventures. This merit badge helps Scouts develop physical fitness, outdoor skills, and an appreciation for nature while learning how to plan and execute hiking trips.

Requirements

  1. Physical Preparation
    • Complete a physical examination
    • Develop a fitness plan
    • Build hiking endurance
    • Learn proper nutrition for hiking
  2. Hiking Skills
    • Learn proper hiking techniques
    • Understand pace and rest stops
    • Practice proper foot care
    • Learn about hiking hazards
  3. Equipment and Clothing
    • Learn about essential gear
    • Understand proper clothing
    • Practice packing a backpack
    • Know how to maintain equipment
  4. Navigation
    • Learn to read maps
    • Use a compass
    • Understand GPS devices
    • Practice route planning
  5. Safety and First Aid
    • Learn hiking safety rules
    • Understand first aid for hiking
    • Know how to handle emergencies
    • Practice Leave No Trace principles
  6. Hiking Trips
    • Complete five 10-mile hikes
    • Complete one 20-mile hike
    • Plan and lead a hike
    • Document your experiences

Pro Tip: For requirement 6, start with shorter hikes and gradually increase distance. Plan your hikes in advance, check weather conditions, and always hike with a buddy. Keep a hiking journal to track your progress and experiences.

Pro Tips

  1. Start Small
    • Begin with short hikes
    • Gradually increase distance
    • Build endurance slowly
    • Listen to your body
  2. Proper Preparation
    • Plan routes in advance
    • Check weather conditions
    • Pack appropriate gear
    • Study the terrain
  3. Safety First
    • Always hike with a buddy
    • Stay on marked trails
    • Carry emergency supplies
    • Know your limits
  4. Leave No Trace
    • Pack out all trash
    • Respect wildlife
    • Stay on trails
    • Minimize impact
